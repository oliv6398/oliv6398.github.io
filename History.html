History of the School

This section is about the school I attend and the one you will vistit. There will also be a short explanation of the Danish school system and what a day of a danish student looks like.

The History

Rysensteen Gymnasium is a high school in Copenhagen. It was founded in 1881 and was back then called "Laura Engelhardts Skole" In the beginning the school was at Stormgade 16. In 1895 it moved to Rysensteensgade 3 and in 1919 was the ownership passed to the commune of Copenhagen and was renamed to Rysensten Gymnasium. In 1932 the school moved again, this time to Tietensgade. Today the school is still at Tietensgade, but there are more than just the main building. The school are located 2 other places as well on Flæsketorvet 60 and in something named "Stalende" in Kødbyen. If you want to know more, you can click the "Learn more" to go to the schools website

The Danish School System

In the Danish school system you start in elementary school when you are around 5 or 6 years old. Here you start in something called 0. grade. This make a natrual transition between kindergarden and school. You start in first grade after that and usually stays at the samme school until you finish 9. grade. This means that eventhough you finish grade 9, you have still attended a school for 10 years. After that you can choose your path, but most people choose to go to high school. There are different kinds that specializes in different things, Rysensteen is what you can call an ordinary high school. You go to high school for 3 years, and you can then go to college.

A Day of a Danish Student

The day of a Danish student usually starts around 7am. First period starts at 8.15am, and the transporttime varries from 5-50 minutes, depending on where you live. The periods last 1.5 hours, and after first period there are a break on 15 minutes. Then we have another period and then lunch. After that we have 2 periods and then we are of at around 3.25pm.
